Data to Noise

Data to Noise is a shift of perspective from the watcher to the watched; it speaks about how our digital selves both reflect and deflect our true selves, and how we can live authentically in a digital world. This work approaches the idea that individuals cannot truly be embodied within a program or series of experiences, no matter how extensive or accurate. When our images and words become nothing more than profitable noise, what really matters beyond the noise is our own sense of individuality.

This work expresses the plights of data collection and digital selves. Data is lost when simplified and expounded upon again and again, until there is little information left, and the rest is filler. This speaks to the supposed vastness of the internet in which information is simplified to such small amounts that when we absorb it, we can only recite so much of it in the end. So, as data becomes more openly available and scrounged for, and our online presences are being processed, we too are lost in the noise of the internet. Simplified and overprocessed until we exist as tensors to inform the collection of more data. However, the work is intended to act as a reminder that as human beings, our existence is more than what we put on the internet. It is more than what we tell others, and it is more than what we tell ourselves. Our existences are their own summary, and any simpler of a description is false. But it is tempting to ask yourself, if every descriptor, every category, and every activity you’ve ever applied to yourself were compressed into a being, how would that being differ from what you consider to be your deepest, truest self?
