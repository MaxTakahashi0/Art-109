Arden

I wanted to emulate the handpainted textures of games like WoW and LoL and so for this model I studied the artwork of Yuriy Porubov and Bernardo Cristovao, senior artists at their respective studios.

I looked to Yuriy's artwork to study skin shading and cloth because I wanted to get a better understanding of how to paint implied subsurface scattering and different materials in this style. Check out his work here: Hearsteel - Kayn

The main thing I studied from Bernardo's piece is how to paint gold, and the wear-and-tear that comes with it inevitably being dented after battle. (In this character's case, they throw their scarf clasp to the side pretty carelessly when they're not using it. Having giant skeleton arms isn't easy, you know?) Here's Bernardo's piece: WARCRAFT | EMBERS OF NELTHARION

My biggest challenge for this project was creating a color scheme that fit the original concept art. Initially, I went for a neon green/plauge theme (Which I absolutely want to make in the future), however since this was a personal work, I figured it would be best to round out my experience by making a saturated and balanced texture. It was a challenge to keep everything saturated yet cohesive, but in the end, I really like how everything pops rather than falling flat. It's low poly, rigged, and ready to raise chaos!
